GuideEyes

Project Overview

This project presents a start-up journey at Monash University in Melbourne as part of the course Technology and Innovation for start-ups. We were exposed to the most current emerging deep technologies including AI, IoT, machine learning and bots, and the challenge presented was to design an innovative product that leverages one of these technologies and influences human lives. The project adopted a design-driven innovation perspective and demonstrated how a human-centred lens is applied when developing new products, services and technologies.

My group collaborated to develop a prototype of AI-powered glasses for individuals suffering from dementia. Leveraging Artificial Intelligence and wearable technology, the glasses aim to enhance the quality of life for dementia patients by assisting with memory recall, cognitive support, and overall well-being.

Process

Research and Brainstorming

Recognizing the struggles of individuals suffering from dementia, the project started with a comprehensive blend of market and user research within the field. We delved into prior solutions and conducted phone interviews with dementia helplines to identify market gaps and user needs. Thereafter, we brainstormed possible solutions that would utilize AI and move beyond already existing ones. We researched the technological capabilities of different wearable solutions and AI and conducted additional phone interviews to discuss various features.

Conceptualization and Design

Having gathered insights, we concluded that wearable glasses stood out as the most appropriate solution, allowing many promising features in one product. We started conceptualizing the design and features and created a Lo-Fi prototype of the glasses in InDesign and a simple companion app in Canva. Additionally, we created images of different use cases from the perspective of the glasses, as seen at the top of this page.
